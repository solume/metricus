Case study: How we helped a B2B business with its AI visibility.

A $15M B2B manufacturer selling into a regulated buyer category. Strong Google rankings, organic search traffic intact, marketing team experienced.

We ran 224 buyer scenarios across major AI systems. The brand surfaced on 30% of buyer queries — but only 2% of awareness-stage queries (buyers just hitting the pain) versus 46% of comparison-stage queries (buyers narrowing options). Voice share among direct competitors: 8% (versus a category leader at 36%).

The audit traced the gap to six specific structural issues: ambiguous brand entity (the brand name collided with an unrelated category), product pages written in internal vocabulary rather than buyer vocabulary, missing pages for the buyer's funding/procurement context, JavaScript-rendered pricing that AI couldn't read, and competitor-displacement queries where the rival won on a single well-structured page.
