What a Snapshot looks like in practice.

A $15M B2B manufacturer selling into a regulated buyer category. Strong Google rankings, organic search traffic intact, marketing team experienced.

We ran 224 buyer scenarios across major AI systems. The brand surfaced on 30% of buyer queries — but only 2% of awareness-stage queries (buyers just hitting the pain) versus 46% of comparison-stage queries (buyers narrowing options). The report sequenced fixes across phases A through I — robots.txt and llms.txt corrections shippable in a day, three new landing pages with full slug/title/H1 specs, JSON-LD schemas for every product page, and a measurement plan for day 30, 60, 90.

FAQ

Questions people ask before ordering.

What if my competitors also do this? Will it still work?
If every competitor in your category runs an AI visibility audit, the result is what happened with SEO: it stops being an edge and becomes table stakes. The first movers compound. The last movers pay more to catch up. Nobody who did it regrets it. The honest answer to the saturation concern: do it sooner rather than later — it's important to do it now.
Why not a $29/mo subscription tool, or $499/mo enterprise tools?
Those tools are monitoring dashboards. They show you data over time — useful when you're running active optimization campaigns and need weekly feedback. The Snapshot is different: a one-time diagnostic that tells you why the data looks the way it does and what to do about it. Most B2B SMBs need the diagnostic first, before they know what they'd be monitoring for. If after the Snapshot you're running active campaigns and want continuous tracking, you will be able to make a much more informed decision about your ongoing needs.
There's a free AI visibility check on other sites. Why pay for one?
Free checks typically run one query against one platform, or use pre-computed references. We use our purposefully designed diagnostic algorithm, with AI expert in the loop. The Snapshot runs 200+ scenarios across the major AI systems, then traces every answer to its source. You're paying for the sample size, the tracing, and the implementation code that follows from it — in combination with an expert review.
Should I even care about AI search as an acquisition source?
Depends on your buyers. If they research with category vocabulary (spec sheets, procurement criteria, certifications, comparison queries), AI search is increasingly where that research starts — above the Google links. For B2C impulse categories or businesses whose buyers find them through ads and referrals, AI search matters less for now. The Snapshot is sized for the first group. If you're in the second, save your $499.
How long does AI search take to update after I ship the fixes?
Most AI systems refresh indexed pages within days, though some can take a few weeks. The Snapshot includes a measurement plan for day 30, 60, and 90, and explains how to re-trigger indexing if changes haven't propagated.
My marketing team says they know about this and are working on it. Why pay $499?
Most marketing teams know AI search matters and don't have a ranked, prioritized, implementation-ready plan for their specific site. The Snapshot is the plan. It doesn't replace your team — it gives them a sequenced todo list with copy, schemas, and metadata they can ship without doing the discovery work themselves.
Is this a one-time thing, or do I need to repeat it?
The fundamentals AI search needs change slowly. Most brands need a re-scan every 6–12 months, or when something significant changes (rebrand, new product line, competitive shift). The Snapshot is structured so you can re-run it later against the same buyer-scenario set and measure delta.
Who reviews the report?
Every report is manually reviewed by our human experts with decades of AI research industry experience.
