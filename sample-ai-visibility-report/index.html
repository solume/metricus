The headline finding

A 20× mention gap to the top competitor — and it isn't a product problem.

The brand has the right product for the buyers AI was answering. Every time AI did surface it, the recommendation language was strong: "the strongest fit", "the best option for one-time work", "ranked #1". The problem is that the brand only surfaces when the buyer's question is phrased very specifically. Loosen the wording one notch and AI never finds it.

Section 9

What makes AI recommend the brand — not just mention it.

Across the 7 mentions in the audit, the strongest predictor of "recommended" vs "merely cited" was the number of pages AI fetched before answering. When AI read multiple pages from the brand's site, it had enough evidence to elevate from citation to recommendation.

This is the single highest-leverage finding in the report. It says: don't write more pages on more topics. Make the 4 pages AI already reads link to each other with the same exact anchor text, repeat the same fact block on each, and AI will start recommending instead of just citing.
