Prioritized Action Plan

1.
Add Hotel and Restaurant schema markup to every page
AI crawlers can't read this hotel's website — room types, pricing, and dining details are loaded via JavaScript. Adding Schema.org Hotel, Offer, and Restaurant structured data in plain HTML gives AI platforms machine-readable facts to cite. This is the single highest-impact fix.
2.
Get listed on 3–5 "best boutique hotels in ████" articles
AI platforms heavily weight third-party "best of" lists and travel editorial. The top competitors appear in Condé Nast Traveller, Time Out, and TripAdvisor roundups. Getting included in even 2–3 of these would give AI a source to cite when recommending hotels in this area.
3.
Publish a comparison page: "Why choose ████ over ████"
The competitors AI recommends all have comparison content that frames their unique angle. This hotel has no equivalent. A well-structured comparison page that positions the hotel against the 2–3 most-recommended competitors gives AI a direct narrative to extract.
4.
Create an FAQ page answering the exact questions travelers ask AI
AI pulls answers from FAQ pages with matching question structure. Publishing answers to "best boutique hotel in [area]," "hotel with restaurant [area]," and "[hotel] vs [competitor]" in FAQ schema gives AI ready-made answer fragments.
5.
Build a reviews and press page with structured citations
AI trusts cited sources. A dedicated page aggregating press mentions, guest reviews, and awards — with proper Review and AggregateRating schema — gives platforms proof points to include alongside competitor mentions.
