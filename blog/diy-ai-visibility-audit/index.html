DIY AI Visibility Audit: Free Method vs Professional Report Compared

Check your brand's AI visibility readiness.

Last updated: April 2026

Metricus built a free AI visibility report readiness check that tells you whether your site is technically prepared for AI search engines. It examines whether AI crawlers can access your content, whether your site is structured for AI citation, and whether your brand facts are findable by the models that power the major AI platforms.

The check covers five categories. Each one addresses a different layer of AI readiness.

The five readiness checks

  • 01

    AI Crawler Access

    Whether GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and other AI crawlers are allowed to reach your site via robots.txt.

  • 02

    llms.txt Configuration

    Whether your site provides a structured summary file that AI models can read to understand your brand, products, and key pages.

  • 03

    Schema Markup

    Whether JSON-LD structured data gives AI models machine-readable facts about your organization, products, and content.

  • 04

    Content Citability

    Whether your pages contain self-contained answer blocks, structured facts, and quotable content that AI can extract and cite.

  • 05

    Sitemap & Technical Foundation

    Whether your sitemap is valid, current, comprehensive, and properly referenced for AI discovery.

This checks whether AI can see your site. But technical readiness and actual visibility are different things. A site can have perfect infrastructure and still be invisible in AI recommendations.

Perfect scores, invisible brands

When we ran a Metricus AI visibility report on a B2B SaaS company with near-perfect readiness scores, the measured data told a completely different story:

67% overall visibility — but invisible to an entire buyer segment

The company appeared in broad comparison queries (92%) but was mentioned in only 18% of industry-specific queries. An entire category of buyers never saw the brand.

AI cited four factual errors about the company

Pricing was wrong. A discontinued feature was described as current. Two competitor comparisons used outdated data. These hallucinations were traced to specific third-party sources.

Third-party sources outweighed company content 6:1

AI pulled from G2, Reddit threads, and a Gartner mention — not the company’s own site. Perfect schema markup didn’t change which sources AI preferred.

Wording mismatch was the #1 visibility killer

The company called their product a “platform”; buyers asked AI about “tools.” The site used “analytics”; queries used “reports.” No technical check catches vocabulary gaps.

FAQ

What does the free AI readiness check cover?

Five areas: AI crawler access (whether GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot can reach your site), llms.txt configuration (the emerging standard for AI-readable site summaries), JSON-LD schema markup (machine-readable identity), content citability (whether your content is structured for AI to quote), and sitemap and technical foundation.

What’s the difference between this and a Metricus report?

This checks whether AI can access and parse your content — the technical infrastructure. A Metricus AI visibility report queries actual AI platforms with real buyer prompts to measure whether AI does mention your brand, what it gets wrong, and how you compare to competitors. The readiness check tells you if the door is open; Metricus tells you who walks through it.

Can a site pass all readiness checks but still be invisible to AI?

Yes. Technical readiness and actual visibility are different things. A site can have perfect robots.txt, valid schema, and strong content structure — and still be invisible because the brand lacks third-party mentions, has no presence on platforms AI models cite (Reddit, Wikipedia, review sites), or uses vocabulary that doesn’t match how buyers ask questions.
