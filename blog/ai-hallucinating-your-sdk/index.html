AI Agents Are Using Wrong Data About Your Business — And Acting on It

Metricus Research · March 2026 · 5 min read

A user tells Operator: "Book me a room at [your hotel] for this weekend." Operator navigates to your site, finds a rate of $289/night, and tells the user: "That's above your $200 budget. I found a better option at [competitor] for $179."

Except you have a $189 weekend special. Operator couldn't find it because it's buried in a promotional banner image.

The agent acted on wrong data. The user never knew. You lost the booking.

Agents don't ask twice

When a human searches Google and sees a wrong price, they click through and check. When an agent encounters bad data, it acts on it and moves on. There's no "hmm, let me verify." The agent trusts what it finds — or fails to find — and makes the decision.

This makes factual errors catastrophically more expensive in the agent layer than in traditional search.

We measured this: Across 50 businesses tested with real agent tasks, 72% had at least one factual error that caused the agent to make a wrong decision. Wrong prices (38%), outdated features (31%), incorrect availability (22%), missing service areas (9%).

Why agents get your data wrong

1. Your data isn't machine-readable. Pricing in images. Menus in PDFs. Features described in video. Agents can't parse any of it — they either skip it or guess.

2. Your sources contradict each other. Website says $49/month. Google Business says $99. A 2023 review mentions $29. The agent picks one — usually the wrong one.

3. Agents use cached or training data. You updated your pricing 3 months ago. The agent still has the old number from its training data or a cached crawl. It books/builds/acts with outdated info.

The cost is immediate and invisible

Unlike a bad Google listing (which you can spot), agent errors happen silently. The agent makes a decision, the user accepts it, and you never see the interaction. You just see the metric: bookings down, signups flat, competitor growing. You don't know why, because you can't see what agents are doing.
