Profound Alternative: Complete AI Visibility Audits Without Enterprise Pricing or Sales Calls

Metricus Research · March 31, 2026 · 11 min read

Profound (formerly known as Brandwatch AI or by its stealth-mode name) is a venture-backed AI visibility platform used by roughly 10% of the Fortune 500. It offers continuous monitoring, consumer panel data through its "Prompt Volumes" feature, and SOC 2 Type II compliance. Pricing starts at $499/month for ChatGPT-only tracking. This article compares Profound's enterprise monitoring model to Metricus's pay-per-report approach — and helps you decide which is right for your budget and use case.

What Profound does well

Profound has raised more than $155 million in venture funding and carries a valuation north of $1 billion. That capital didn't come from nowhere. The company has built a genuinely differentiated product for a specific class of buyer — large enterprises that need continuous AI visibility monitoring with compliance-grade infrastructure. Before we compare, it's worth understanding what Profound does better than almost anyone else in this space.

Prompt Volumes — real consumer panel data. This is Profound's most unique feature and the one no competitor has replicated. Prompt Volumes draws on actual consumer panel data to show brands how many real people are asking specific questions to AI platforms. Think of it as keyword volume data for AI search. Traditional AI visibility tools (including Metricus) can tell you whether your brand appears for a given prompt, but Profound can tell you how many consumers are actually typing that prompt. This is genuinely valuable for prioritization. If you're deciding between optimizing for "best CRM for startups" versus "best CRM for real estate agents," knowing which prompt has 10x the consumer volume changes your strategy. No other tool in the market offers this demand-side data today.

Shopping Visibility for e-commerce. Profound has built specific tooling for e-commerce brands that want to track how their products appear in AI shopping recommendations. As more consumers use ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI features to research purchases, this vertical-specific coverage matters. If you're running a DTC brand or managing product listings for a retailer, Profound's Shopping Visibility module gives you data that general-purpose AI visibility tools don't surface.

SOC 2 Type II compliance and enterprise security. For Fortune 500 companies and regulated industries, security compliance is a gate, not a nice-to-have. Profound holds SOC 2 Type II certification, which means their data handling, access controls, and operational processes have been independently audited. If your procurement team requires SOC 2 before approving any vendor, Profound clears that bar. This single fact eliminates most competitors from enterprise shortlists.

Platform breadth at Enterprise tier. On their Enterprise plan, Profound covers 10+ AI platforms. They track ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and several other AI surfaces. For organizations that need comprehensive monitoring across every major AI platform, the Enterprise tier delivers genuine breadth.

Strong market traction. Profound serves roughly 10% of the Fortune 500, according to their public materials. On G2, they carry a 4.6 out of 5 rating with 140+ reviews. That kind of adoption and review volume provides social proof that the product works at scale. When you're evaluating vendors, a 4.6-star rating across 140+ reviews is a meaningful signal — it means the product is delivering consistent value to a large customer base, not just a handful of early adopters.

Where Profound falls short

Profound's strengths are real, but they come with tradeoffs that matter significantly for teams outside the Fortune 500. The product was built for enterprise buyers with enterprise budgets, and that shows in both the pricing and the purchase experience.

Lite plan: $499/month for ChatGPT only. Profound's entry-level plan costs $499 per month and covers a single AI platform — ChatGPT. You get 50 tracked prompts and 1 user seat. For context, that's $499/month to monitor what one AI chatbot says about your brand for 50 queries. If you want to know what Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, or any other platform says, you need to upgrade. At this price point, you're paying nearly $6,000 per year for partial visibility into one platform.

Growth plan: $399/month for 3 platforms. The Growth tier expands coverage to 3 AI platforms, but still at a substantial monthly commitment. While the per-platform cost drops compared to Lite, $399/month remains a significant line item for mid-market companies, agencies, and startups. And three platforms still leaves gaps — if you care about what Claude, Grok, DeepSeek, Copilot, and Google AI Overviews say about your brand, you're still not getting the full picture.

No free trial and no self-serve signup. You cannot try Profound without talking to a sales representative. There is no free tier, no trial period, and no way to enter a credit card and start using the product today. Every potential customer must go through a sales process. For teams that just want to understand their AI visibility landscape quickly, this creates a multi-day (sometimes multi-week) delay before you see any data. Compare this to tools where you can sign up, pay, and get results within hours.

1-3 week implementation before any data. Even after you sign the contract, Profound requires an implementation period. The platform needs to be configured for your brand, your competitors, your tracked prompts, and your reporting requirements. This implementation phase typically takes 1 to 3 weeks. If you're a marketing director who just got asked by the CEO "what do AI chatbots say about us?" you can't get an answer from Profound for potentially a month after you first reach out — between the sales cycle and implementation.

Enterprise pricing: $2,000-5,000+ per month. For the full Profound experience — 10+ platforms, multiple seats, Prompt Volumes, Shopping Visibility, dedicated support — you're looking at Enterprise pricing that ranges from $2,000 to $5,000+ per month. At $24,000 to $60,000+ per year, this is firmly in the territory of enterprise marketing budgets. The product delivers genuine value at this tier, but the cost structure excludes the vast majority of businesses from accessing it.

The real cost over 12 months

Pricing looks different when you annualize it. Most businesses reviewing AI visibility tools are thinking in annual budget terms, so here's what each option actually costs over a year.

Profound Lite: $5,988 per year. That's $499 times 12 months for ChatGPT-only tracking with 50 prompts and 1 seat. You get continuous monitoring of a single platform, which is valuable if ChatGPT is your only concern. But $5,988 for one platform is a steep annual commitment, especially when you don't know what Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Grok, DeepSeek, Copilot, or Google AI Overviews are saying about your brand.

Profound Growth: $4,788 per year. At $399 per month, the Growth plan extends coverage to 3 AI platforms. The annual cost is slightly lower than Lite (different pricing structure), but you're still spending nearly $5,000 per year and still missing 5+ major AI platforms.

Profound Enterprise: $24,000-60,000+ per year. The full-featured Enterprise plan, with 10+ platforms, Prompt Volumes, Shopping Visibility, and dedicated support, runs $2,000 to $5,000+ per month. This is the tier where you get everything Profound offers — and where the product genuinely shines. But the budget requirement puts it firmly in Fortune 500 territory.

Metricus quarterly audits: $396-1,996 per year. If you order a Metricus report once per quarter — which is a common cadence for teams that want to track progress without continuous monitoring — your annual cost ranges from $396 (four Snapshot reports at $99 each) to $1,996 (four Full Arsenal reports at $499 each). Every report covers all major AI platforms, includes source attribution, and comes with a prioritized action plan. At the high end, four Full Arsenal reports per year still cost less than half of Profound Lite's annual price.

To put it plainly: the annual cost of Profound Lite ($5,988) would buy you 60 Metricus Snapshot reports or 20 Deep Dive reports or 12 Full Arsenal reports. We're not suggesting you need that many reports. We're illustrating the scale of the cost difference.

When Profound is the better choice

Profound isn't overpriced for the buyers it was built for. If you match the following profile, Profound is likely the right tool.

Fortune 500 companies with dedicated AI visibility teams. If you have a team (or even a single full-time hire) whose job is to manage your brand's presence across AI platforms, Profound's continuous monitoring, trend dashboards, and alert system give that person the tooling they need to do their job effectively. The cost is a rounding error on a Fortune 500 marketing budget, and the enterprise features (SSO, role-based access, SOC 2 compliance, custom SLAs) meet procurement requirements.

Organizations that require SOC 2 Type II compliance. If your company policy or industry regulation mandates SOC 2 Type II certification for all data vendors, Profound is one of the very few AI visibility tools that clears this bar. This isn't a feature preference — it's a hard procurement gate. If you need it, you need it.

Teams that need consumer panel data (Prompt Volumes). If your strategy depends on knowing which AI prompts have the highest consumer volume — not just whether you appear, but how many real people are asking — Prompt Volumes is a genuinely unique capability. No other tool offers this. If demand-side data is central to how you prioritize AI optimization work, Profound is currently the only option.

E-commerce brands that need Shopping Visibility. If you sell physical products and need to track how AI platforms recommend your products in shopping contexts specifically, Profound's vertical-specific tooling is purpose-built for this use case. General-purpose AI visibility tools, including Metricus, don't offer the same depth of e-commerce-specific tracking.
