We Analyzed 20 Mid-Size Companies for Agent Readiness. Most Were Sitting on Untapped Gold.

Metricus Research · March 2026 · 6 min read

We took 20 mid-size companies — SaaS, e-commerce, professional services, fintech — and analyzed them entirely from the outside. Their websites, public tool stacks, support channels, hiring pages, and industry patterns.

The question: where could AI agents have the highest impact for each company?

73% had high-impact opportunities they hadn't considered

Most companies we analyzed were thinking about AI agents for the obvious use case: customer support chatbots. But the highest-ROI opportunities were elsewhere.

Invoice and expense processing (avg. 12 hours/week saved). Sales follow-up automation (avg. 30% faster response time). Internal knowledge retrieval (avg. 8 hours/week saved per team). These were hiding in plain sight — companies just hadn't connected the dots.

The pattern: Companies underestimate what they already have. Your CRM data, support tickets, and internal docs are often enough to power high-impact agents. You don't need new infrastructure — you need someone to show you what to connect.

The data you're sitting on

Every company we analyzed had at least 3-4 rich data sources that agents could use immediately:

Support tickets: Years of customer questions and answers — perfect training data for a support agent.

CRM records: Customer history, deal stages, communication logs — everything a sales agent needs.

Internal docs: Processes, policies, product specs — the knowledge base your team already searches manually.

Email/Slack history: How your team actually communicates and decides — patterns an ops agent can learn from.

Why companies get stuck

The problem isn't technology. It's knowing where to start. Companies see a hundred possible AI use cases and freeze. Or they start with the wrong one — a flashy chatbot instead of the boring-but-high-impact invoice processor — and conclude "AI doesn't work for us."
